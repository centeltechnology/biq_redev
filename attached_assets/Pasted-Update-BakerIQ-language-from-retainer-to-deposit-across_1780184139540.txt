Update BakerIQ language from “retainer” to “deposit” across onboarding and dashboard UI.

Do not change Stripe logic, payment logic, database behavior, or routes. This is a copy/label update only unless a variable name is purely display-related.

Reason:
“Retainer” feels too formal for home bakers and treat makers. “Deposit” is clearer, more familiar, and better aligned with how bakers already talk to customers.

---

## 1. Onboarding Step 7

Find the final Stripe/payment onboarding step.

Replace:

“Enable retainers”

with:

“Accept deposits automatically”

Replace any similar text like:

“Customers can pay retainers online.”

with:

“When customers approve quotes, they can pay deposits online.”

Update Stripe card copy to:

“Connect Stripe so customers can pay deposits when they approve quotes. You’ll receive payouts directly to your bank account.”

If an example payment breakdown is shown, use:

“Example:
Quote total: $192
Deposit: $50
Balance due later: $142”

Update the main button text from:

“Enable Retainers”

to:

“Accept Deposits”

Update small helper text to:

“You can do this now or later. Quotes still work without deposits enabled.”

“Activate secure payments and start receiving deposits”

that is fine.

But avoid using “retainer” anywhere in the main baker-facing flow.

Preferred language:

“Collect deposits”
“Customers can pay deposits online”
