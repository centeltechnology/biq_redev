Clarify and Upgrade Analytics Structure Without Mixing the Two Dashboards

Important: There are two different analytics surfaces in the admin, and they must remain separate.

1. Existing Analytics Surfaces
A) Admin Tab: “Analytics”

This is the business / product analytics dashboard inside the Super Admin dashboard.

It currently contains sections like:

Activation Funnel

Revenue Health

Retention Snapshot

Tier Distribution

Growth Trend

This area is for business performance and product activation metrics.

Do not mix landing page funnel metrics into this dashboard.

B) Separate Page: /admin/analytics

This is the Site Analytics page reached by clicking the “Site Analytics” button from the Analytics tab.

This page is for marketing / landing page analytics only.

It contains metrics like:

Visitors

Page Views

Calc Visible

Calc Uses

Calc Interactions

CTA Clicks

Signup Clicks

Accounts Created

Conversion Rate

This page should remain focused on landing page traffic and conversion behavior.

Do not mix business metrics like GMV, Stripe, retention, or platform revenue into this page.

2. Naming Clarification

To reduce confusion, update labels in the UI:

In the Super Admin dashboard:

Change the Analytics tab label from:

Analytics

to either:

Business Analytics

or

Product Analytics

Pick the one that best fits existing design.

For the button linking to /admin/analytics:

Change the label from:

Site Analytics

to one of these:

Marketing Funnel

Page Analytics

Landing Page Analytics

Preferred label:
Landing Page Analytics

The goal is to make the distinction obvious:

Business Analytics = product/business performance

Landing Page Analytics = marketing funnel performance

4. Keep the Two Dashboards Linked but Clearly Separate

From the main admin Analytics tab, keep a button linking to /admin/analytics.

But visually make it clear this is a separate destination.

Suggested helper text under the button:
View landing page traffic and conversion funnel
